## What I do

**I design at AI speed, and I can show the method.** The EF Impact Library was a multi-page portal that I designed in six days, against eight source documents that kept changing every few days. Claude Code and the Figma MCP carried the production work. The document-driven loop I ran it on is the part worth copying.

**I build what I design.** My graphic portfolio site is hand-coded, with no framework, and it runs on Cloudflare Pages. On FoodCare I built the screens as [real HTML, CSS and JS](https://dianature.github.io/foodcare-case-study/prototype/FoodCare.dc.html) before opening Figma, because code puts pressure on a design system in a way a mockup never does. A token either exists or it does not.

**I work in complicated, multi-role systems.** For the Journalism Trust Initiative I redesigned a certification platform used by 1,700 media outlets across 85 countries, where people were dropping out mid-certification because they could not tell what state they were in. For the UNESCO MAB platform I mapped a nine-step approval process across four roles and 100 countries, on a no-code build with almost no UI flexibility. Constraints like that are normal in my work.

**I treat accessibility as a design constraint from the first screen.** I worked on the IQTIG patient survey portal, where the people filling in the form are patients and some of them are disabled. In FoodCare I carried the same rule into the safety system, so a verdict is shown by a word, an icon and a shape together and never by colour alone. It survives greyscale, and it survives colour blindness.

**I run research and let it change the answer.** For FoodCare I interviewed 14 people, transcribed the audio with Whisper, and read the chat surveys by hand. The findings closed the brief I had been given. I wrote that down and changed the product. The easy move would have been to quietly design what I had planned to design anyway.

**I write my own tools.** When I catch myself making the same correction by hand, I write the correction down as a Claude skill. [voice-applier](https://github.com/dianature/voice-applier) came out of that habit and is public.

**I still make things by hand.** Brand and motion came first for me, and they hold the rest up. The OuiRace mascots started as Midjourney and Leonardo generations and came out of Blender as animation. For a Geneva Call campaign in Myanmar I produced the visuals with Gemini and Seedance, because there was no photo budget and the footage did not exist.
